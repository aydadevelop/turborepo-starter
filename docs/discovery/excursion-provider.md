# Role: Excursion Provider / Guide

## Mental Model

I am selling expertise, time, and reputation.

I do not think of myself as “configuring a record”.

I think in:

- my profile
- my route or story
- my schedule
- group format
- ticketing and pricing
- orders to confirm
- reviews and reputation

## Core Job To Be Done

Help me publish an attractive excursion and run it reliably with minimal operational friction.

## Key Questions

- How do I create a convincing excursion?
- What information is required for publication?
- Why was my excursion approved or rejected?
- How do I control my calendar and schedule?
- How do I change prices or discounts?
- What do I need to confirm when a booking arrives?
- How do I protect my rating and reliability?

## Trust Triggers

- clear onboarding and publication checklist
- understandable moderation feedback
- visible readiness state
- clean order status model
- easy schedule/calendar controls
- pricing tools that match how guides actually sell

## Failure Triggers

- unclear moderation reasons
- hidden publish blockers
- confusing calendar controls
- price setup that feels like accounting instead of selling
- unclear order confirmation obligations
- poor visibility into customer-facing result

## Objects This User Thinks In

- guide profile
- excursion
- schedule/calendar
- availability block
- price
- discount
- booking/order
- review
- moderation feedback

## Happy Path

1. Set up guide profile.
2. Create excursion.
3. Upload photos and description.
4. Configure format, schedule, and price.
5. Pass moderation.
6. Receive and manage bookings.
7. Build review reputation.

## Playwright Exploration Missions

### Mission 1: publish first excursion

- Can I understand what is needed to publish?
- Do I know what quality bar I am being held to?

### Mission 2: adjust operations

- Can I change schedule, price, or discount without fear?
- Can I see how those changes affect bookability?

### Mission 3: manage an incoming order

- Can I tell whether I need to confirm, message, or do nothing?
- Are order statuses understandable?

## What To Inspect In Our Product

- onboarding and readiness surfaces
- listing creation and moderation flow
- description/photo guidance
- schedule and availability editors
- discount and pricing ergonomics
- order operations and status presentation

## First Red Tests We Should Eventually Have

- provider can understand why a listing is not publishable
- provider can configure schedule and see resulting bookability
- provider can confirm or update an order without ambiguity
